Measuring the Pulse of Prosperity: An Index of Economic Freedom Analysis

Introduction

Economic freedom is the fundamental right of individuals to control their own labor and property. In a truly free economy, people are free to work, produce, consume, and invest as they choose. Governments in economically free societies support this by minimizing restrictions and allowing the free flow of labor, capital, and goods—intervening only to uphold the rule of law and protect individual liberty.

Historically, most of the world has lacked economic freedom, trapping billions in poverty and limiting opportunity. But today, we live in the most prosperous era in human history—thanks in large part to the advancement of economic freedom.

The 2022 Index of Economic Freedom, published by The Heritage Foundation, evaluates the principles that fuel global prosperity. It serves as a powerful annual guide to how freedom is creating better lives across the world.

The index scores countries based on 12 indicators, grouped into four main pillars:

  • Rule of Law: property rights, judicial effectiveness, government integrity
  • Government Size: government spending, tax burden, fiscal health
  • Regulatory Efficiency: business freedom, labor freedom, monetary freedom
  • Open Markets: trade freedom, investment freedom, financial freedom

About

This project brings economic freedom data to life through interactive Tableau dashboards and engaging data stories. Our goal is to offer an intuitive platform that helps users explore trends, compare country performance, and understand the key drivers of economic prosperity.

By combining clear visualizations with reliable data, we aim to inform policy discussions and inspire action toward a freer, more prosperous global economy.
